Process

Why I keep coming back to marbles

At its core, a marble is just a sphere. That's it. No corners, no edges, no complex structure to engineer. And yet that simplicity is exactly what makes it so endlessly fun to work with.

When the form is fixed, the only variable left is decoration — and that's where the real exploration begins. Color placement, layering techniques, encasing, fuming, implosions — there are infinite ways to dress up a sphere. Every marble becomes a little experiment. You set up your variables, apply heat, and see what the glass decides to do. Sometimes it surprises you. Sometimes it humbles you. But it's never boring.

That's what keeps me coming back. Not the mastery of it — but the exploration. The marble is just the canvas. The fun is in seeing what shows up inside.
