I've been making things with my hands for as long as I can remember — first out of necessity, then out of obsession. The studio started as a corner of a spare room and grew into something I never expected: a real practice, with real people who care about the work.

My background is in [your background here], and the influence shows up in everything from the color choices to the way I approach a blank surface. I work primarily in [your medium], though the materials are always changing depending on what the piece needs.

The philosophy

I'm not interested in making things quickly. Every piece takes the time it takes, and I think that shows — in the texture, the layers, the small decisions that accumulate into something worth keeping.

I believe art should live in homes, not just galleries. Pieces here are priced fairly and made to be part of someone's actual life — hung where the light is good, next to the things you love.
