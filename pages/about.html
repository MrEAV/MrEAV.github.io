A little about me

I discovered lampworking in one of the least artistic places imaginable: an Army staff duty shift. Somewhere between the quiet hum of flourescent lights, I stumbled down a YouTube rabbit hole of glassblowing videos and that cracked something open in me. The world of glass- the colors, the heat, the precision, the dedication, the Freedom. - felt like a universe i didnt know id been missing

Glass is a formidable medium. It is tolerant, yet can be unforgiving. Working it feels like playdough turning into honey. to me its math in motion, complete focus. A balancing act of variables like heat gradients, timing, chemistry, gravity, air pressure, and intuition all at once. It is a craft that reflects you back at yourself. If you’re anxious, angry, or unfocused, the glass will show it. And when you’re focussed and in the zone, it becomes a kind of symphony: flame, breath, movement, and then suddenly a finished piece in your hands, like waking from a trance.

The philosophy

After several years of steady glass work, the trial and error taught me valuable life lessons. I had started creating for money instead of for the love of the art. That shift drained the joy out of the process. The pressure to produce killed the spark that got me into glass in the first place. Its my outlet, my therapy, my meditation.

Now, after three years away, I’m returning with new intentions. I want to make what I feel like making — pieces born from curiosity, not obligation. I want to create for creations sake and if someone connects with a piece enough to want it, that’s a bonus. Honestly, I’d give most of my work away if I could, but I’m still figuring out how to balance passion with sustainability. This is a new chapter for me. A return to the flame with a different mindset: less noise, more honesty. More exploration. More joy. Stay true to yourself. -ALL One Love-
