⚠ Safety Notice — Read Before Building

Pure oxygen is an aggressive oxidizer. Oil, grease, hydrocarbons, or even fingerprints on fittings can ignite violently in an O₂-enriched stream — sometimes with no warning and no ignition source. Every component in this system (compressor, tank, regulator, fittings, hose, thread sealant) MUST be rated and certified oil-free for oxygen service. Do not substitute standard shop-air parts. When in doubt, consult a qualified gas systems professional.

① Parallel O₂ Source

4x concentrators on 2-tier wire rack feed a common manifold. At ~10 LPM each, combined output is roughly 40 LPM of 90%+ O₂.

② Buffer Tank OPTIONAL

Low-pressure reservoir decouples concentrator flow from compressor draw. Helps prevent starving the concentrators and dropping O₂ purity. Can be omitted if the compressor duty cycle stays within concentrator output.

③ Oilless Compressor

Gast oilless — critical for O₂ service. Any oil in pure O₂ creates severe fire/explosion hazard. Pulls from buffer (or directly from manifold), pushes to storage.

④ Storage Tank (80 gal)

High-pressure reserve. Provides sustained working volume for the torch so the compressor can cycle rather than run continuously. Must be clean and oxygen-service rated.

⑤ Regulator

Step-down from tank pressure to working torch pressure. Must be O₂-rated (no oil/grease on seats).

⑥ Flashback Arrestor ⚠

CRITICAL safety device. Stops flame front from traveling upstream into the tank if the torch backfires. Install as close to torch side as possible.

⑦ Foot Pedal Valve

Hands-free on/off control at the workstation. Lets the operator work the torch without manually valving.

⑧ Torch ⚠

Point of use. Oxy-fuel head — pair with appropriate fuel gas line (not shown on this side of the schematic).

Oxygen service is inherently hazardous. Improperly built or maintained O₂ systems can rupture, ignite, or explode — causing serious injury, death, or property loss.
